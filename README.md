# Student Club Event Registration Analysis

An analysis of my annual student club event registration data to identify promotion effectiveness and inform future event strategy.

## Key Findings
- Registration peaked in late April, driven almost entirely by a spike in staff introductions — suggesting a concentrated but unsustainable promotional push
- Nearly 50% of registrations came through staff referrals, creating over-dependence on a single unscalable channel that cannot be replicated consistently year-on-year
- Social media (Instagram, Facebook, X) and the club website contributed minimally to registrations
- Instagram is the strongest digital channel for university students, showing consistent daily registrations. Facebook, while low in overall volume, disproportionately attracts working professionals — making it worth developing as a targeted channel for that demographic.
- Institution referrals proved effective for attracting high school students, with uptick in late April coinciding with the start of the academic and work calendar

## Recommendation
Conclusion : The club's promotion strategy is heavily reliant on word-of-mouth and personal invitation; shifting investment toward SNS and website presence is recommended to build sustainable, scalable reach
- Reduce dependency on staff referrals by building a structured digital promotion calendar — prioritizing Instagram for university students and institution partnerships for high school outreach. 
- Discontinue train station advertising and invest to growing website traffic and SNS presence.

## Tools Used
- Python, pandas, matplotlib, Streamlit, SQLite

## Data
Anonymized registration data from a student club annual event. Personal information removed prior to analysis.
