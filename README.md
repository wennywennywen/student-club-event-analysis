# Student Club Event Registration Analysis

An analysis of student club event registration data across 2025 and 2026 to identify promotion effectiveness, track year-over-year growth, and inform future event strategy.

## What This Project Does
- Cleans and loads raw Japanese registration data into SQLite
- Analyses acquisition channels, attendee demographics, and registration trends
- Compares 2025 vs 2026 registration growth, channel performance, and attendee composition at the same point in time (Day 47)

## Key Findings: 2025 Analysis
- Registration peaked in late April, driven almost entirely by a spike in staff introductions — suggesting a concentrated but unsustainable promotional push
- Nearly 50% of registrations came through staff referrals, creating over-dependence on a single unscalable channel
- Instagram is the strongest digital channel for university students. Facebook, while low in volume, disproportionately attracts working professionals
- Institution referrals proved effective for attracting high school students, with an uptick in late April coinciding with the start of the academic calendar

## Key Findings: 2025 vs 2026 Comparison (Day 47)
- Homepage traffic up 125% — digital channels significantly more effective in 2026
- Instagram down 51%, Facebook down 84% — social media reach declining
- Institutional referrals down 76% — school/company referrals nearly gone
- Working professionals now nearly equal to university students in 2026, suggesting a more professional audience
- 2026 registration pace tracks closely with 2025 in early weeks — a late push strategy will be critical to match 2025's final numbers

## Recommendation
The club's promotion strategy is heavily reliant on word-of-mouth and personal invitation. Shifting investment toward digital channels is recommended to build sustainable, scalable reach.
- Reduce dependency on staff referrals by building a structured digital promotion calendar — prioritizing Instagram for university students and institution partnerships for high school outreach
- Discontinue train station advertising and invest in growing website traffic and SNS presence
- Double down on homepage — the only channel showing significant growth in 2026

## Tech Stack
Python, pandas, matplotlib, Streamlit, SQLite

## Data
Anonymized registration data from a student club annual event. Personal information removed prior to analysis.
